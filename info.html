The Palace is not Safe

THE PALACE IS NOT SAFE is a photographic series documenting fire extinguishers within museums and exhibition spaces around the world.

The title references the Crystal Palace in London — the great exhibition hall built for the 1851 World's Fair — which was destroyed by fire in 1936. Despite its grandeur, the building lacked adequate fire suppression. The blaze consumed the structure entirely.

Today, museums house irreplaceable works of art. The series asks: are these palaces of culture truly safe? By turning the camera away from the exhibited art and toward the fire extinguishers — those overlooked, mundane objects tucked into corners and behind doors — the work challenges how we see exhibition spaces, what we consider worthy of attention, and the (in)visible systems that protect what we value most.

Each photograph is paired with the name of the museum, the exhibition on view at the time of the photograph, and the dates of that exhibition. The fire extinguisher becomes a silent witness to the art it guards.

The series is ongoing.
